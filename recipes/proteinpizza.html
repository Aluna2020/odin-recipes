Protein pizza

Do you consider yourself a pizza lover like me?! of course you are who doesn't love pizza right, that's why you are here. We all know that constantly eating pizza day in and day out is generally not healthy for you, but what if i was to tell you theres an healthier and alternative way to have your pizza. this is a easy preparation with simple ingredients as well, so take out your notepad and take notes as you read along this blog to create your own tasty pizza. I promise it is not a long preperation and cooking time, the plan here is to enjoy the goodness with little time to waste!

Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup self rising flour
  • 1/4 cup fat free greek yogurt plain
  • 1/4 teaspoon yeast
  • 1/2 chicken sausasge cooked
  • 1/4 cup fat-free cheddar cheese
  • 3 tablespoon fat free feta cheese(optional)
  • 1/4 cup pizza sauce
  • veggies of choice (spinach, brocoli, mushrooms)

Steps

  1. pre-heat oven to about 475 degrees
  2. mix flour, yeast, along with the greek yogurt until it becomes a dough ball
  3. now put the dough ball into an oil sprayed parchment paper, place another paper on top and use a rolling pin until it's flattened out
  4. now you may add your toppings onto your pizza, then place pizza onto a pre-heated sheet pan do not remove parchment paper)
  5. last but not least place the pan into the oven, and lower the oven to 450 degrees, and bake for about 8 minutes. Voila ! your pizza is ready to eat.

    High protein pizza

    ingredients
    • 142 grams bread flour
    • 55 grams soy flour
    • 11 grams flour
    • 175 grams of water
    • 4 grams of salt
    • 1 gram of yeast
    • 30 grams san marzano sauce
    • 1/2 chicken italian sausage link (sliced)
    • 90 grams of mozzarella
    • basil leaves after pizza comes out the oven
    • olive oil brush for after baking
    Steps for mixing the dough
    1. Set a bowl over a scale. Add the flours, water, instant yeast, and salt. Mix with your hands or a stand mixer fitted with a dough hook until no dry flour remains, about 2 minutes. If kneading by hand, cover with plastic wrap and let rest for 15 minutes. Once rested, or if using a stand mixer continue kneading the dough until it becomes smooth and passes the windowpane test. Let rest for 15 minutes.
    2. Cover bowl with plastic wrap, and preferably, let cold ferment in the fridge for 24 to 120 hours (1 to 5 days). If cooking pinsa the same day, perform three sets of stretch and folds by folding the four edges of the dough up and back to the center, turning the dough over, and letting rest for 30 minutes before repeating 2 more times.
    3. At least 2 hours before you are ready to bake, separate the pizza dough into 5 equal pieces, roughly 185 g each. Pull the edges of the dough into the center and form a tight ball. Sprinkle some rice on your work surface or a baking sheet and place the ball on it. Repeat with each dough piece then sprinkle some more flour over top before covering with another baking sheet, plastic wrap, or a towel until doubled in size.
    4. Once almost doubled (about 2 hours), spread the dough into roughly an oval shape by using your fingertips to gently stretch and pull until roughly 12 inches long and 6 inches wide.
    Steps for baking
    1. preheat the oven the highest it will go, likely 500 to 550 F. Place the shaped pizza on an aluminum baking screen and add the toppings of your choice. Once preheated, bake until evenly browned and crisp. Depending on the oven this could be 4-8 minutes.
    2. Let cool a bit before slicing and enjoy.
